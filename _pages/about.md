Taehwan (Theo) Jung is a materials scientist specializing in the epitaxial synthesis of complex quantum materials and freestanding crystalline membranes by Molecular Beam Epitaxy (MBE). His research focuses on (1) understanding and controlling epitaxial growth mechanisms, particularly at graphene-terminated and van der Waals interfaces, and (2) engineering emergent quantum phenomena, including high-temperature and topological superconductivity, through strain and interface engineering. He integrates in-situ growth monitoring (RHEED) with comprehensive ex-situ characterization — including AFM, SEM, Raman spectroscopy, XRD, STM, and XPS — as well as low-temperature transport and magnetometry (PPMS, SQUID), to connect growth kinetics, interfacial structure, and functional properties.
